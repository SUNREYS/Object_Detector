EVALUATION RESULTS — WHAT EACH VALUE MEANS
Thesis: Pedestrian Detection on Synthetic Thermal-to-Visible Translations
==============================================================================

This file explains every number in the evaluation output, in plain language.
All examples reference the PID modality results.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 1 — OVERALL DETECTION PERFORMANCE
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

GT objects (14 689)
    Total number of annotated pedestrians in the validation set.
    This is the ground truth — every person the detector SHOULD find.

True Positives — TP (5 536)
    Detections that correctly matched a ground truth box (IoU ≥ 0.5).
    "The detector found this pedestrian."

False Positives — FP (825)
    Detections that did NOT match any ground truth box.
    "The detector thought it saw a pedestrian, but there was none."

False Negatives — FN (9 153)
    Ground truth boxes that no detection was matched to.
    "The detector missed this pedestrian entirely."

Precision (0.8703)
    Formula: TP / (TP + FP)
    Of every detection the model made, 87% were correct.
    High precision = detector rarely cries wolf.

Recall (0.3769)
    Formula: TP / (TP + FN) = TP / GT objects
    Of every real pedestrian, the detector found only 38%.
    Low recall = detector misses many people. This is the key weakness here.

F1 Score (0.5260)
    Formula: 2 × Precision × Recall / (Precision + Recall)
    Harmonic mean of precision and recall. Summarises overall performance
    in one number. 0 = worst, 1 = perfect.
    High precision + low recall → F1 is pulled down toward the worse metric.

SECTION 2 — DAY / NIGHT / SIZE RECALL
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Recall day (0.3782) / night (0.3740)
    Recall computed separately for daytime and night-time images.
    Near-identical here → lighting condition does not strongly affect PID.

Recall small (0.1795) / medium (0.3566) / large (0.4627)
    Recall split by object size category:
      small  = bounding box area < ~32×32 pixels
      medium = typical pedestrian size
      large  = close-up / large bounding box
    Small objects are hardest: fewer pixels → less visual information for
    the detector. The 0.18 recall for small objects vs 0.46 for large
    confirms the expected size-difficulty relationship.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 3b — GHOST & HALLUCINATION METRICS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

These metrics compare the synthetic (generated) image against its thermal
ground truth. They measure how faithfully the generator reproduced the
original thermal scene.

ghost_score  (image-level mean 0.0608 / per-object mean 0.0613)
    Measures structured residual error between the generated and thermal image.
    How: pixel-by-pixel absolute difference, then Gaussian blur to suppress
    noise, then mean. Range 0–1.
    Low = generated image is close to thermal (good fidelity).
    High = visible structured differences / ghosting artifacts.
    0.06 is relatively low → PID generation has limited ghosting.

hallucination_score  (image-level mean 0.3190 / per-object mean 0.3417)
    Fraction of edges in the generated image that have NO corresponding edge
    in the thermal ground truth.
    How: Canny edges on both images; thermal edges are dilated (tolerance);
    hallucinated = gen edges not covered by dilated thermal edges.
    Range 0–1. 0 = every generated edge matches reality; 1 = all edges are fabricated.
    0.34 means ~34% of generated edges don't exist in reality — the generator
    invented visual features. This is moderate hallucination.

object_ssim  (image-level mean 0.6358 / per-object mean 0.6360)
    Structural Similarity Index (SSIM) between a generated object crop and the
    corresponding thermal GT crop. Per-channel average over BGR.
    Range –1 to 1; 1 = structurally identical.
    0.64 means moderate structural preservation — generated objects look roughly
    like their thermal counterparts but with noticeable differences.

By day / night:
    Day ghost_score higher (0.069) than night (0.046): more ghosting artifacts
    in daytime images, possibly due to sunlight/thermal contrast differences.
    Night object_ssim higher (0.751 vs 0.575 day): nighttime generated images
    are structurally closer to thermal — thermal imagery is more predictable at night.

Detected vs missed objects:
    Detected objects have lower ghost_score (0.042) and higher object_ssim (0.719)
    than missed objects (ghost 0.073, ssim 0.586).
    → The detector performs better when the generated image closely resembles
    the thermal ground truth. Poor generation quality directly causes misses.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 3c — LPIPS (PERCEPTUAL SIMILARITY)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

lpips_mean  (0.1786 image-level / 0.1757 per-object)
    Learned Perceptual Image Patch Similarity between generated and thermal crops.
    Uses deep features from pretrained AlexNet. Range roughly 0–1.
    0 = perceptually identical; higher = more perceptually different.
    0.18 = low-to-moderate perceptual difference.

object_lpips detected vs missed:
    Detected: mean 0.1127   Missed: mean 0.2149
    Objects with higher perceptual error (LPIPS ≈ 0.21) are twice as likely
    to be missed. The detector is sensitive to perceptual quality: if the
    generated pedestrian doesn't "look right" perceptually, it gets missed.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 5 — CORRELATION WITH DETECTION PERFORMANCE
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Only four key metrics are shown. Each row shows the Pearson correlation
between that metric and three performance measures (recall, F1, mean_conf).

How to read the table:
    r > 0 → higher metric value → better detection performance
    r < 0 → higher metric value → worse detection performance
    * = statistically significant (p < 0.05)
    No * = might be due to chance

sharpness   r ≈ +0.04 (recall), +0.04 (F1), +0.07 (conf)
    Weak positive: sharper images slightly easier to detect.
    Significance reached but effect size is small.

ghost_score   r ≈ –0.25 (recall), –0.25 (F1), –0.38 (conf)
    Moderate negative: more ghosting → significantly lower detection.
    Ghost_score is among the strongest predictors of poor detection here.

object_ssim   r ≈ +0.30 (recall), +0.31 (F1), +0.49 (conf)
    Moderate positive: higher structural similarity → better detection.
    Strongest single correlation with confidence (0.49*).
    When the generated image structurally resembles the thermal original,
    the detector is both more accurate and more confident.

lpips_mean   r ≈ –0.30 (recall), –0.30 (F1), –0.46 (conf)
    Moderate negative: higher perceptual error → worse detection.
    Mirror of object_ssim; confirms the perceptual quality finding.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 6 — SHARPNESS × SIZE RECALL TABLE
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Cross-tabulation of recall by sharpness level and object size.
Sharpness = Var(Laplacian) on the object crop; higher = sharper edges.
Split at the median: "low" = below median, "high" = at or above.

How to read:
    Each cell = recall(detected/total) for objects in that (sharpness, size) cell.
    (N) in parentheses = number of objects in that cell.

Example (Overall):
    low sharpness / small:   0.07  (182 objects) — 7% recall, very poor
    high sharpness / small:  0.58  (52 objects)  — 58% recall, much better
    low sharpness / large:   0.43  (2154 objects)
    high sharpness / large:  0.54  (1040 objects)

Key insight: Sharpness matters most for small objects. The gap between
low (0.07) and high (0.58) sharpness recall is huge for small objects,
but smaller for medium (0.30 vs 0.40) and large (0.43 vs 0.54).
This confirms the hypothesis: small objects have few pixels, so blur
destroys the discriminative features the detector needs.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 7 — FALSE POSITIVE ANALYSIS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

FPPI  (False Positives Per Image)
    FPPI = total FP / total images.
    Standard metric for evaluating false alarm rate.
    Lower is better — an autonomous system must handle each false alarm.

FPPI by day/night:
    Separate FPPI for daytime and night images.
    Higher FPPI during the day suggests more background clutter / complex
    scenes confusing the detector during daylight hours.

High-confidence FP note:
    Percentage of FPs with confidence > 0.5.
    Low-confidence FPs (0.25–0.5) are expected near the threshold and less
    concerning. High-confidence FPs indicate the detector is strongly misled
    — e.g., a textured patch that closely resembles a person both visually
    and in the generation domain.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 8 — PERFORMANCE BY CONDITION (LOW/HIGH SPLIT)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

For each metric, images are split at the median into "low" and "high" halves.
Recall and F1 are computed for each half (overall, day, night).

Columns:
    Low Recall   = recall for images where the metric is below the median
    High Recall  = recall for images where the metric is at or above median
    Low F1 / High F1 = same, but for F1 score
    N low / N high   = number of images in each half

How to read:
    If High Recall >> Low Recall → high values of this metric help detection.
    If Low Recall >> High Recall → high values hurt detection.

Sharpness:
    High sharpness recall > low sharpness recall → sharper images easier to detect.

Hallucination score:
    High hallucination recall > low hallucination recall (counterintuitive).
    Caveat: images with hallucination_score = 0 are often perfectly dark/blank patches
    where Canny finds no edges at all (score defaults to 0), not genuine quality.
    The raw split must be interpreted with care; the correlation table
    (+0.05 with recall) is a better guide — effect is weak.

Object SSIM:
    High SSIM recall (0.585) >> low SSIM recall (0.194) → strongest condition split.
    Confirms: structural similarity to thermal GT is the dominant factor.
    For night images, the gap is even more extreme (0.44 vs 0.017).

LPIPS mean:
    Low LPIPS (= better perceptual quality) recall (0.622) >> high LPIPS (0.139).
    Objects that perceptually resemble the thermal GT are detected at 4.5×
    higher rate than those with large perceptual error.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 9 — ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS (LOGISTIC REGRESSION)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

A logistic regression model is trained to predict whether each object is
detected (1) or missed (0) from per-object features.

Features are standardised (mean=0, std=1) before fitting, so coefficients
are directly comparable as importance scores — larger absolute value = more
important, sign indicates direction.

Model accuracy (0.8339)
    83% of objects correctly classified as detected/missed on training data.
    High because the class imbalance (many misses heavily favour predicting
    "missed"), so interpret with care. The coefficient ordering matters more
    than the accuracy number.

Coefficient interpretation:

    object_edge_strength  +2.70  →  helps ↑
        Most important feature. Objects with stronger edges are much more
        likely to be detected. Edge-rich objects (clear outlines, high
        contrast boundaries) are easy for the detector to recognise.

    object_contrast       –1.23  →  hurts ↓
        Higher local contrast around the object actually correlates with
        misses. Likely a confound: high contrast regions may include
        background clutter, not the object itself.

    object_lpips          –0.95  →  hurts ↓
        Higher perceptual error → more missed detections. Confirms the
        generation quality finding — badly generated pedestrians get missed.

    object_blur           –0.88  →  hurts ↓
        Higher Laplacian variance (= sharper crop) coded as "blur" here,
        but note: this is actually the sharpness score with inverted sign
        in the naming. Blurrier objects (low variance) → harder to detect.
        (Check naming: object_blur = Var(Laplacian); higher = sharper.)

    object_ssim           +0.81  →  helps ↑
        Higher structural similarity to thermal GT helps detection.
        Confirms the key finding: generation fidelity → detection success.

    object_brightness     –0.44  →  hurts ↓
        Brighter objects slightly harder. May reflect overexposed or blown-out
        crops in the translation where thermal hot-spots become white regions.

    bbox_area_px          +0.41  →  helps ↑
        Larger objects easier to detect, as expected from Section 2.

    ghost_score           +0.18  →  helps ↑  (weak, near 0)
        Weak positive — likely a suppressed effect because ghost_score
        and object_ssim are correlated; in isolation ghost_score hurts.

    fg_bg_diff            +0.09  →  helps ↑  (very weak)
        Objects that stand out from background (high foreground–background
        brightness difference) slightly easier to detect.

    hallucination_score   +0.04  →  helps ↑  (negligible)
        Effectively zero — hallucination does not independently affect
        detection once other features are controlled for.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SUMMARY: KEY TAKEAWAYS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. Recall is low (0.38): the model misses ~62% of pedestrians in PID images.
   Precision is high (0.87): most detections ARE correct, so the problem is
   coverage, not false alarms.

2. Generation quality is the dominant failure driver:
   — object_ssim and lpips_mean are the strongest correlates of detection
     success (|r| ≈ 0.30–0.49).
   — Detected objects have ~73% SSIM vs ~59% for missed objects.
   — Detected objects have LPIPS 0.11 vs 0.21 for missed.

3. Object size matters more than day/night:
   — Small objects: 18% recall, large: 46%.
   — Day vs night recall is nearly identical (0.378 vs 0.374).

4. Sharpness has a gating effect for small objects:
   — High sharpness / small: 58% recall.
   — Low sharpness / small:   7% recall.
   — Effect is much weaker for larger objects.

5. FPPI is low (see Section 7): the detector is conservative — it prefers
   to miss people rather than hallucinate them.
